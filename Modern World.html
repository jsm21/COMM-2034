Overview

History of the Modern World

History of the Modern World falls under CLE Area 7: Critical Issues in a Global Context. The course focuses on a wide range of global events, from the Trans-Atlantic slave trade to World War II. The course looks at how these historical events have impacted the world we live in today. In the class, we study topics and themes such as colonialism, capitalism, slavery, nationalism, and much more. The goal of the class is to get students to "think like a historian and to critically assess the past."

My Teacher: Taulby Edmondson

My professor, Taulby Edmondson, earned a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in History and Sociology from Emory & Henry College in 2010. He then obtained a Master of Arts (M.A.) in History at Appalachian State University in Boone, North Carolina. He considers himself a scholar of race and nationalism, and much of his research focuses on the memory politics of slavery and the Civil War in the modern United States, especially the "Lost Cause" movement. An aspiring social scientist, Professor Edmondson is currently working on his Ph.D. here at Virginia Tech. He has been a member of the VT community since 2012.

Professor Edmondson is extremely well-versed in all things relating to U.S. and even world history, and it shows in his lectures. His presentations move along fairly quickly, and he does not include much of what he says on his powerpoints. Therefore, it is up to the student to focus in class so they can retain the information presented. Thankfully, I already had familiarity with Professor Edmondson as I took his Civil War class in the Spring of 2018. He is an excellent teacher and will make an effort to answer students' questions in as much detail as he possibly can.

My Thoughts

History of the Modern World is a classs that is very heavy on details. As I noted before, it is very important to pay attention to the lecture in class because most of the important details mentioned will not appear on a screen. The bulk of the course grade comes in the form of two items. First, there are eight Canvas quizzes throughout the semester. To do well on these quizzes, you must read the supplementary handouts on Canvas and the required texts. There is no "easy way out". Second, we completed a five-page Critical Book Report (CBR) in early November, which accounts for 35% of our grade. While this book report was very challenging for me, I spent a significant amount of time and energy into my essay and I felt it turned out very well. I have always been a fan of history class going back to elementary school, so it is not surprising to me that I was so motivated to do well.
